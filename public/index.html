Karaoke and Game Night

Our Karaoke event is hosted at K-mix.

This event is a great way to experience the more "fun" part of Chinese culture as it will feature popular games in China that include cultural elements. As for the games that we have brough to the past events, Mahjong may spark off interest to learn to count in Chinese as the game revolves around gathering the sequence of numbers. Another game, Sanguosha, is a card game based on the context of the period of Three Kingdoms so that participants can learn some Chinese history while having fun.

Mid-Autumn Festival Celebration

Reunite with your best friends.

Mid Autumn Festival is a very important cultural festival for the Chinese culture. Traditionally, Chinese people get back home no maYer how far away they are reunite with their families, celebrate with food and drinks (especially mooncakes) on this night, while enjoying the beautiful full moon. Each year, CSA will invite all undergraduates to join us and celebrate this festivity with us together.

CSA will try to bring the traditional celebration on campus. We will be serving mooncakes and some authentic traditional Chinese dishes and snacks. Traditionally, we have had a huge turnout for this event; every year the number of people showing up has been over 100.

Academic Workshop

Learn more about life at Duke, learn more about life with CSA.

You'll find a lot of information, skills, and tricks that you will need at Duke during the workshop. A number of upperclassmen are invited to speak on different aspects of Duke life including academics, extracurricular activities, life, entertainment and MORE.

It will also be a good opportunity to mingle with CSA officers if you are interested in joining the CSA executive board. A brief overview of CSA will be given before the workshop, and a mingle session will be held right after

Paddle Under the Stars

Beautiful sky.

Floating in a kayak at night with stars peppering the crystal clear night sky, the moon reflecting on the water’s surface, and a legit Professor of Astrology from UNC pointing out “the seven sisters”, “Orion’s Belt”, and artificial satellites revolving the earth with a laser pen that literally "shoots" to the sky in the night, and telling us about the astronomy, myths and legends of Egyptians, Mayans and Indigenous people of Americas.

It's gonna be a good study break from all the midterm preps, and a great night having fun, bonding with fellow CSA members, while learning some serious canoeing skills.

Hotpot Night

Out annual free hotpot event.

Hot-pot dinner has been a beloved tradition in China for a long time, and it'd be an opportunity for students across cultures to interact with one another by sitting together at large round tables and taste some "hot" food. CSA is proud to organize this event periodically, each time with a greater turnout than the previous one.

Mentor-Mentee Program

Find a mentor/ mentee with similar interest and career path.

As a cherished CSA tradition, the CSA mentor-mentee program helps freshmen better connect with the Duke community. We will assign each mentee (freshman) with a mentor (upperclassman) based on academic interests among other factors, and mentors offer advice on all aspects to mentees throughout the year. We highly value this program and strongly encourage everyone to participate.

At the beginning of each school year, we will ask participants to fill out a survey. You can find a sample survey from last year here.
